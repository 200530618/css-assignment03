About Marshmallow

Marshmallow is a soft and fluffy candy made from sugar, water, and gelatin. It has been enjoyed for thousands of years and comes in a variety of flavors and shapes, including classic vanilla, strawberry, and chocolate. Marshmallow can be enjoyed on its own or used as an ingredient in many recipes, such as s'mores, hot cocoa, and rice krispie treats.

The History of Marshmallow

The original marshmallow was made from the root of the marshmallow plant and was used for medicinal purposes by the ancient Egyptians. In the 19th century, French candy makers started making marshmallows using egg whites and sugar, which became the modern version of the candy we know today. Since then, marshmallows have become a popular treat around the world.
